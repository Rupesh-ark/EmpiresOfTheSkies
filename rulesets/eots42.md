# EMPIRES OF THE SKY

## Rulebook

## BACKGROUND STORY

Bound by the deadly cold of the Icy Waste to the north, snow-capped mountains to the east, the hostile Infidel Empire to the south and the stormy Ocean Sea to the west, the peoples of Faithdom have often wondered what lies beyond. Could it be the Land of Faerie, filled with strange beings and fabulous wealth? Or fiery dragons, tentacled sea monsters and other legendary creatures and places? The exotic goods smuggled from the Infidels hint at such. But in the Six Kingdoms they only whisper such things, for here the Church of the Faith brands any who utter such pagan fancies a heretic. Only in the Two Republics, where no absolute ruler enforces the will of the Prelacy, is there more freedom to speak and think. And there it is that alchemists have discovered the secret of turning water into "lifting air", geographers have calculated the roundness and extent of the undiscovered world and shipwrights have devised vessels that use lifting air to fly — SKYSHIPS. Already bold aviators are venturing on voyages of discovery, disproving the Orthodox dogma that there is nothing beyond but sea and sky.

You are the Monarch of a kingdom, beset with the troubles of state. Pledged (whatever your private doubts) to punish any sign of heresy in your Realm, in return the Church sanctifies your reign. You must also be ready to supply troops for — and perhaps lead — the Grand Army of the Faith to counter the ever-present menace of the Infidel Empire, whose fanatic hordes may sweep over all Faithdom unless you and your fellow rulers unite to stop them. Gold raised by taxes can scarcely keep up with expenditure.

But now there are these new-fangled skyships, and captains at court seeking patronage, promising to fly to newfound lands and bring back their treasures to fill your coffers. Purchase skyships from the Republics' shipwrights, or establish your own shipyards to build them. Send fleets of these to claim outposts and establish profitable trade monopolies. Send bigger fleets filled with soldiers to conquer these distant lands and obtain even more of their wealth — but beware territorial ambitions on the part of your fellow Monarchs, for they (and you) may consider the Peace of the Faith not to apply beyond Faithdom: probably you should garrison and fortify your new possessions. Establish factories to dominate the outgoing trade to the new lands. Use these profits to outspend your rivals and achieve the most glorious reign — the biggest empire, the mightiest military, the most cathedrals and palaces — and seek to control the Church, by having your candidate elected Archprelate. Crush the heretics who increase as discoveries of strange peoples and legendary encounters progressively undermine Church dogma; or turn heretic yourself, crushing believers instead and proclaiming your supremacy through magnificent palaces — the new cathedrals — but possibly at the price of being cursed by the Church. Ultimately leave behind a formidable legacy when your reign finally ends.

Everything is possible while the gold continues to flow… but what's this? Knavish skydogs swooping on your trade routes, stealing your hard-won treasure! There is but one answer to such brazen piracy — war! Prepare your aerial armada…

## PHASE 1: EVENTS

Events are random occurrences affecting either all players or one or two selected players, each specified on an Event Card. Each Event Card is laid out in the following format: title, subtitle, circumstances in which to ignore the card and draw another, which player(s) it applies to, and what it does. If the instructions of an Event Card would violate the main rules, the card takes precedence.

### Events Phase Procedure

1. Each player chooses and lays one Event card face-down in front of them from their hand (of three).
2. Gather these cards and shuffle them.
3. Pick the top card and reveal it.
4. Resolve the picked card, either immediately or at the time indicated by the text.
5. If that card would have no effect, pick another until a relevant card is revealed or they run out, in which case there is no event.
6. Leave the relevant card face-up on the table; shuffle the rest back into the Event deck.
7. Each player draws one Event card to replenish their hand.

### Battle Events

If a card bears the "Battle" symbol (crossed swords on a shield) then it is enacted during the Resolution Phase. An Event-mandated rebellion within a Kingdom is resolved before any other battles in the "Resolve Rebellion" Stage. An Event-mandated battle elsewhere ("Faerie Uprising", "Infidels Invade Faerie" or "Colonial REBELLION") is resolved during the "Resolve Ground Battle" segment of the "Resolve Encounters" Stage, prior to any other Ground Battle waged in the same map tile. In the case of "Faerie Uprising", a rival Fleet or Fleets in the affected map tile with no other rival Fleets present may add their force to the Land: the Land absorbs losses first.

### Rebellion Rules

If an Event title contains the word "REBELLION" then the following applies in common:

1. All Rebellions are decided in the "Resolve Rebellion" Stage, **except** the "Colonial REBELLION" Event which is decided in the "Resolve Ground Battle" section of the "Resolve Encounters" Stage.
2. Draw one Rebels counter — this is the internal rebel force to which external pro-rebel Swords can be added.
3. The selected player decides what forces, if any, to commit to fighting the rebels — these must be Regiments, Levies and/or Fleets present in their Kingdom or other location of the rebellion. If the player elects to commit no forces, then the rebellion succeeds without fighting — ignore the remaining steps (4) to (6). In the case of "Colonial REBELLION", any defending garrison goes back to its Kingdom, leaving any Fort behind intact.
4. If the rebellion is within a Kingdom, rival players may each contribute up to **three** Regiments or Levies to either side — Kingdom or Rebel. Each such player IPO declares which side they support, or abstains, and how many Regiments or Levies (if any) they wish to commit. The selected player may refuse help. In the case of the "Colonial REBELLION" Event, any assistance must come from a Fleet or Fleets in the affected map tile with no rival Fleet present, and all of its force may be used.
5. Battle follows. Battle rules as for attacking an Outpost/Colony apply: the rebel side count as the attackers and must eliminate all the defenders to win, otherwise the rebellion fails. If there is a Fort, it adds one Shield per defending Regiment that is not aboard a Fleet. No VPs are awarded for a rebel victory.
6. Rebel losses fall first on the Rebels counter, then on their allies in order of most to least size, ties resolved IPO. Defender losses fall on the selected Kingdom or its allies in order of most to least size, ties resolved IPO. Surviving external forces go back to their Kingdom, or their Fleet(s) in the case of "Colonial REBELLION".

## PHASE 2: DISCOVERIES

Starting with the first player, IPO each player may flip one hidden tile of their choice, but which must be in edge contact with the Known World or a revealed tile.

**Chain Flipping:** When an Ocean or Legend tile is revealed, flip another tile in edge contact with that last tile, or any revealed tile if there are no more hidden tiles next to the last tile, until a Land tile is revealed or there are no more hidden tiles. A player may instead choose not to flip a tile.

**If a Legend tile, or a Land tile of a so far undiscovered Race, is revealed, all players advance their Heresy track markers one space.**

Each tile when flipped must be oriented so that its text is aligned with that of the Known World.

**East-West Wrap-Around:** The eastern and western edges of the world-space wrap around, so that if a tile has already been revealed at the eastern or western boundary then it is legitimate to flip a tile on the opposite boundary at the same latitude.

## PHASE 3: TAXES

Each player draws Gold from the bank according to their Player Order position, as indicated below it on the Action Board, unless amended by an Event. A player with the "More Efficient Taxation" Kingdom Advantage always adds **two more Gold** to their total.

## PHASE 4: ACTIONS

Players select and perform Actions by placing their Counsellors (cylinder pawns), one at a time IPO, in slots marked on the Action Board or their own Kingdom Boards (and pay any required price), until they pass or run out of Counsellors. They may select them in any order. Each player may place up to **seven** Counsellors on either board during the phase.

### Action Board Slot Types

```
  ACTION BOARD SLOT TYPES
  ═══════════════════════

  ┌─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─┐   DOTTED CIRCLE: Only ONE of a player's
  │     ○      │   Counsellors may be placed in the entire row.
  └─ ─ ─ ─ ─ ─┘

  ┌──┐ ┌──┐ ┌──┐   SMALL SQUARES: Only one Counsellor per slot,
  │  │ │  │ │  │   but a player may place multiple Counsellors
  └──┘ └──┘ └──┘   across different slots in the row.

  ┌────────────┐   LARGE RECTANGLES (blue-striped): Any player
  │  Cost → ☆  │   may place any number of Counsellors in a
  │  (stacking)│   slot, one at a time. Cost INCREASES by
  └────────────┘   +1 Gold for each Counsellor placed there.
```

**Placing of a Counsellor commits a player to fulfilling that Action.** If an Action requires a player to spend more Gold than they have or can (or choose to) raise through Anytime Options, **they must incur the difference as Debt**: place Gold equal to the Debt incurred in the Debt space of their Kingdom board. Whenever a player obtains more Gold from whatever source, **reducing their Debt has first call** on that income: for each Gold obtained remove one from the Debt space to the bank until the Debt space is emptied.

### Actions on the Action Board

**1. Change Player Order** — Players choose new (or the same) slots for their markers on the Player Order track. **Unlike all other actions, this does not take immediate effect — changes happen only at Reset.**

**2. Appoint Counsellors** — Players pay to the bank **one Gold per Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Counsellors slot to add one Counsellor to their set, up to the maximum total of seven. Each new Counsellor is immediately available to use.

**3. Recruit Regiments** — Players pay to the bank **one Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Regiments slot to obtain Regiments four at a time. New Regiments can only be recruited in Kingdoms, and players are limited to a maximum of 30 Regiments each (the number of cubes in their supply). If there are fewer than four cubes in their supply, they must still pay the sum of Gold required by that slot.

**4–5. Purchase Skyships** — Players pay to the bank **two Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in either the Zeeland Skyships or Venoa Skyships slot to obtain Skyships from either republic two at a time. Players are limited to a maximum of 24 Skyships each (the number of discs in their supply). If there are fewer than two discs in their supply, they must still pay the sum of Gold required by that slot.

**6. Found Factories** — All players start with one Factory. To found another, pay to the bank **one Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Factories slot. Advance the Factories marker (grey building) one space upward on its track. Each player may have a maximum of six Factories.

**7. Found Cathedrals** — All players start with one Cathedral. To found another, pay to the bank **five Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Cathedrals slot. Advance the Cathedrals marker (purple building) one space upward on its track. Each player may have a maximum of six Cathedrals. **Heretic Monarchs cannot found Cathedrals.** Each new Cathedral immediately retreats its player's Heresy track marker one space and scores **2VP**.

**8. Found Palaces** — All players start with one Palace. To found another, pay to the bank **five Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Palaces slot. Advance the Palaces marker (gold building) one space upward on its track. Each player may have a maximum of six Palaces. Each new Palace immediately advances **or** retreats its player's Heresy track marker (player's choice) and scores **1VP** for an Orthodox Monarch, **2VP** for a Heretic Monarch.

**9. Found Shipyards** — Pay to the bank **three Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Shipyards slot, to place a Shipyard tile on or near the Kingdom board. Each player may found a maximum of **three** Shipyards, each capable of building one or two Skyships per Round. Prior to founding, keep each Shipyard tile with the pool of pieces, "Shipyard" side up; once founded, flip the tile to "Skyship" side up and place it on or beside its Kingdom board.

**10. Found Forts** — Pay to the bank **two Gold, plus one Gold for each Counsellor** (including the one they have just placed) in the Forts slot to place a Fort building in a Land where you have an Outpost or Colony **and** one or more Regiments or Levies, or in your Kingdom. No more than one Fort may occupy a Land or a Kingdom. Players may not found a Fort unless they have a valid place to put it.

**11. Influence Prelates** — Motivate the Council of Prelates to vote as desired in the upcoming Archprelate election: no cost for a player's Counsellor in their own Kingdom's slot (this serves to prevent another player from bribing those prelates), **one Gold** for a non-player Realm slot, or **Gold equal to its current number of Cathedrals** for a rival Kingdom's slot. If a player's Kingdom slot remains empty, they still control their own prelates' votes. Each Cathedral grants one vote: if between this action and the election the number of Cathedrals in a Kingdom should change, the number of votes changes to match, but not the sum of Gold that might have been paid to obtain them.

**12. Issue Holy Decree** — The Archprelate player (only) may do **one** of the following:

1. **Curse** one Monarch — this must be one who is a Heretic, or if there are no Heretics the Orthodox Monarch whose Heresy track marker is furthest advanced. If two or more Monarchs qualify, the Archprelate chooses. Immediately deduct from the affected player VPs equal to the **number of Orthodox Realms divided by three**, rounded up (maximum 3 VPs).
2. **Bless** one Orthodox Monarch — this must be the Orthodox Monarch whose Heresy track marker is least advanced. **A Heretic Monarch may not be Blessed.** If two or more Monarchs qualify, the Archprelate chooses. Immediately award to the affected player VPs equal to the **number of Orthodox Realms divided by three**, rounded up (maximum 3 VPs).
3. **Reform Dogma** — All players retreat their Heresy track markers one space.
4. **Confirm Dogma** — All players advance their Heresy track markers one space.

**1. Build Skyships** — If a player has founded one or more Shipyards in their Kingdom, **once per Round** for each Shipyard they may build one or two Skyships, paying **one Gold** to the bank for each. Players are limited to a maximum of 24 Skyships each (the number of discs in their supply).

**2. Punish Dissenters** — As an Orthodox Monarch (Heresy track marker purple side up) a player may act **once per Round** to suppress Heresy in their Kingdom, while as a Heretic Monarch (Heresy track marker orange side up) they may likewise suppress Orthodoxy, paying **two Gold** OR **one unspent Counsellor** (returned to its pool).

Players accomplish this by either imprisonment or execution, or both, and so shift their Heresy track marker up (if Heretic) or down (if Orthodox) by **one space per punished Dissenter**, up to a maximum of **three spaces**, or **four spaces** with the "More Prisons" Kingdom Advantage. The Heresy track marker cannot be shifted beyond either end of the track.

For **imprisonment**, place Dissenters (plain wood cubes) in the Prison box of the Kingdom board. Each Heretic on being imprisoned retreats the Heresy track marker one space; each Believer advances it one space. **At most three cubes** can occupy a Prison box at once (four with "More Prisons").

For **execution**, take one or more already-imprisoned cubes and return them to their pool, for each advancing or retreating your Heresy track marker as above. **Each executed Dissenter immediately costs the player −1VP.**

**3. Convert Monarch** — Players who placed a Counsellor in this space **must** now flip their Heresy track marker, from Orthodox (purple side up) to Heretic (orange side up), or vice versa, paying **two Gold** AND **one unspent Counsellor** (returned to its pool). If a Monarch converts, at that instant **all imprisoned Dissenters must be released** (return the cubes to their pool), immediately **reversing** the Heresy track marker shifts achieved by their imprisonment (i.e., advance the Heresy track marker by the number of Dissenters released if converting to Heresy, or retreat it by the number released if converting to Orthodoxy).

**4. Conscript Levies** — Players may obtain up to 12 Levies each (the number in their supply) in groups of three, paying **1VP per group**. If there are fewer than three Levies remaining in their supply, they must still pay 1VP for that final group.

**5. Train Troops** — Players may draw two Fortunes of War cards into their hand, at no cost. If a "No Effect" card is drawn, reveal it, shuffle it and the discard pile into the deck, and draw another card. If a player's hand now exceeds the maximum size of **four**, discard down to four.

**6–8. Dispatch 1st/2nd/3rd Fleet** — A Fleet in a Kingdom may not move unless it has been assigned one or more Skyships. A maximum of **five** Skyships may be assigned to a Fleet. Regiments and Levies can be assigned to a Fleet (**one** Regiment or Levy per Skyship) that are in the same location. A maximum of **three** Fleets may be deployed at one time.

A Fleet may move up to **four** map squares, through their edges or diagonally, including between same or adjacent latitude tiles at the eastern and western boundaries, but **not across mountain ranges unless entirely unladen** (i.e., no Regiments or Levies aboard any Skyship). At each square players may deposit a Skyship if there is none of theirs there, or pick up one previously placed. Likewise, players may transfer Skyships, Regiments or Levies to or from their other Fleets in the same square, or transfer Regiments or Levies to or from their Outposts or Colonies. If a Fleet is completely emptied, the Fleet model is removed from the map to the corresponding Fleet box. Once a player has finished moving a Fleet, **neither it nor any of its Skyships, Regiments or Levies can move again in that Actions Phase, except to evade or retreat**.

**For each map square that a Fleet is moved during the Actions phase the owning player pays one Gold to the bank.**

### Resolve Rebellion Events

See "Rebellion Rules" under Phase 1: Events.

### Resolve Fleet Encounters

In general, encounters are resolved one map square at a time, starting with the most north-east discovered tile and proceeding south, then northernmost west — retrace as necessary if evasions or retreats generate new encounters.

#### a) Resolve Aerial Battles

Where two or more players' Fleets occupy the same map square, aerial battles are possible. If more than one of a player's Fleets are together, they combine in attack and/or defence. Each player IPO has the option to attack one other player whose Fleet(s) is present, whereupon the attacked party can choose either to fight or **evade**. If the choice is to evade, the attacker can challenge another player, and so on until one elects to fight or there are no more players. If there is a fight, the winner is the survivor, or the party that inflicted most losses: the winner then compels the defeated survivors to **retreat** — defenders win ties. Then, if there are still rivals together, the next player IPO has the option to attack. If no-one attacks, rival Fleets may co-exist in the same square.

**Skyships forming Trade Routes may neither attack nor be attacked**: only Skyships in Fleets may attack or be attacked.

**Evasion and Retreat** — Whenever a player offers to attack a rival's Fleet(s) with their Fleet(s), the threatened Fleet(s) may evade battle by moving to a discovered adjacent square, or remaining in the same square, that square chosen by the **attacker**. Likewise, the defeated survivors of an aerial battle must retreat to a square chosen by the **winner**. If an evasion or retreat is to another map square (other than Faithdom), it must be empty of Fleets unfriendly to the evader or retreater: if every adjacent square contains such, then the evasion or retreat is to the nearest empty square. Fleets that evade or retreat may make ground attacks, or later engage in Piracy, in eligible map squares to which they are moved.

**The Infidel Fleet** — This is a Host counter distinguished by a "balloon" symbol and bearing fixed Sword and Shield values. In the Encounter stage, it immediately attacks, IPO, each player's Fleets present in its map square: if these evade or must retreat, their player chooses where they go. If it wins a battle, **its losses are ignored**; if it loses but its losses are less than its strength, the counter remains on the map but is flipped; if its losses are equal or greater, the counter is returned face-down to its pool. While it remains inverted, the flipped counter is **inactive**: it does not fight and does not affect Trade Routes. **It is flipped back at its original strength at the start of the next Encounter Stage**, and immediately attacks Fleets present as above.

**AMENDMENT — Infidel Fleet Targeting:** When the Infidel Fleet counter is drawn and placed on the Infidel Empire map square, and at the start of each subsequent Encounter Stage while it remains face-up, determine its target as follows: Count each player's current military power — the combined total of all their Regiments (each worth two), Levies (each worth one) and Skyships (each worth one), whether in their Kingdom, in Fleets or garrisoned in Lands. The player with the highest total is the Infidel Fleet's designated target for that Round. The Infidel Fleet immediately moves to the map square occupied by the largest Fleet of the designated target player, if any such Fleet is on the map. If that player has no Fleets on the map, the Infidel Fleet remains in the Infidel Empire square and conducts piracy as normal. Once in position, it attacks that Fleet as per the normal Infidel Fleet rules. If the targeted Fleet evades or is destroyed, the Infidel Fleet remains in that square for piracy purposes until the next Encounter Stage. In the case of a tie for highest military power, the Infidel Fleet targets each tied player alternately, beginning with the first tied player in Player Order, advancing to the next tied player each Round.

**AMENDMENT — Infidel Fleet Prior Combat:** If the Infidel Fleet has already engaged in aerial combat during the Encounter Stage of the same Round in which an invasion is triggered, its current state carries directly into the invasion's aerial combat phase: if it was destroyed during Encounters, it does not participate in the invasion's aerial phase at all — proceed directly to ground combat; if it was flipped (i.e., it lost but survived), it remains inactive and likewise does not participate in the invasion's aerial phase; if it won or was unengaged, it participates in the invasion's aerial phase at its full printed strength as normal.

#### b) Plunder Legend

A player whose Fleet(s) is in **sole occupation** of a Legend (i.e., any rival Fleet that ended movement in the same map square has evaded or been destroyed or retreated by battle) may obtain the reward indicated on the tile. Such rewards are not subject to Piracy. If the reward is one Gold plus one Goods cube, move the Price marker on the relevant Sell Goods track one space left.

**For each Legend plundered, the plundering player must advance their Heresy track marker by one space.**

#### c) Resolve Ground Battle

A player whose Fleet(s) is the only such in a Land occupied by a rival's Garrison may attack it. **If any rival Fleet is present, no ground attack may be made.** The attacking player may offer the defender the option to **Yield**: if accepted (and either side has a chain of Skyships linking back to Faithdom), the Garrison units go back to their Kingdom, leaving the Outpost or Colony and any Fort undefended — otherwise there is a battle. To win the battle, the attacker must survive while **completely eliminating** the defending Regiments and/or Levies (i.e., the number of hits inflicted by the attacker must equal or exceed the defender's Strength, while hits inflicted on the attacker must not equal or exceed their Strength). A victorious attacker controls the Outpost or Colony and any Fort, and may go on to Claim the Land: the same is the case of an undefended Outpost or Colony.

**Attack on the Infidel Hosts** — Players may use their Fleets to attack Host counters in the Infidel Empire. If the **Infidel Fleet** is present, that must be fought first in an aerial battle. If able to proceed to ground combat, add together all the non-Fleet Host counters' Swords and pit these against the attacker's total of Swords and Shields. Resolve as above, except that the Infidels never Yield, and the attackers' hits must equal or exceed the value of a Host counter to cause that counter to be removed: Host counters are removed in order of smallest to largest; any remainder hits less than a whole counter are ignored. The attacking player **gains one VP** if any of their Fleet(s) survives, and **gains one VP for each Host counter removed**. Removed counters are flipped and go back to their pool.

#### d) Resolve Conquest Attempt

This requires that the attacker controls the Outpost in the Land to be conquered, if there is one, and has one or more Fleets, or a Garrison, or both, present to make the attack. **If any rival Fleet is present, no conquest attempt may be made.** The defence consists of the Sword and Shield values printed on the map tile. To win the attacker must have survivors and inflict hits equal to or greater than the defending Strength. In that event, place a Colony counter, or flip the existing Outpost counter to its "Colony" side: thereafter the tile's printed Sword and Shield values no longer apply, unless through an Event Card. If the Land already has an Outpost, move the Price marker of the relevant Sell Goods track one space left for each additional Goods cube indicated on the tile (those inside the broken-line rectangle).

If the attack **fails** (i.e., the attacking force is wiped out or inflicts losses less than the defenders' strength), then the inhabitants retain control of the Land. Any Outpost is lost and its counter removed from the map: place its Skyship elsewhere on that map tile. Any Fort is removed from the map; any Regiments or Levies on the ground who cannot be immediately uplifted aboard the surviving Skyships of an attacking Fleet are returned to their pool.

**Conquest-Related Events** — The "Headstrong Commander" Event obliges one player to conduct a conquest attempt in one Land with an Outpost. If a rival player attacks that Outpost, however, this obligation is cancelled. The "Faerie Uprising" Event causes one Land with a Colony to attack its Garrison (and supporting Fleet(s) if present) with **twice** its printed Swords. If the Land wins, the Colony counter and any Fort are removed, only the previous owner's Trade Route Skyship and any surviving Fleet remaining. If a rival Fleet or Fleets are in that Land with no other players' Fleet(s) present, they may add their force to that of the Land: the Land absorbs losses first. Thereafter the same Fleet or Fleets may make a separate ground attack and/or conquest attempt with their survivors.

#### e) Claim Land

A player may claim a Land where their Fleet(s) is present, including one previously claimed but whose owner was defeated in a preceding ground battle. They immediately **score 1VP**. If two or more rival Fleets occupy an unclaimed Land (because there was no aerial attack), the option to claim belongs IPO.

**A player who fails in an attempt to conquer a Land (i.e., turn it into a Colony) may not (re)claim that Land in the same Round.**

**The claiming player must advance their Heresy track marker by one space.**

To claim, place an Outpost or Colony counter on the Land tile, and a single Skyship on top to mark ownership: if a counter is already there, first move the rival Skyship to elsewhere on the map tile. No more than one Outpost or Colony may occupy a Land. In the same action players may disembark Regiments or Levies as a Garrison.

Then the player may place one or more of their Skyships on map squares so as to form a contiguous chain from that Land leading back to Faithdom, through edges and/or diagonally through corners. **Such a chain cannot be traced through edges or corners marked by mountains.** Skyships already so placed can be counted as part or all of the new chain. Players do not have to form such a chain, but failure to do so will prevent them from running a Trade Route from that Land. Newly-placed Skyships may be taken from unassigned ones on Kingdom boards, from Fleets (as long as any Regiments aboard can be otherwise accommodated) or by redeployment from elsewhere on the map. No more than one of a player's Skyships may occupy a map square, and no Skyships can be placed on the Faithdom square itself. Multiple players' single Skyships may coexist in a map square.

If a Trade Route has been established, and if the Land is being claimed for the first time, move the Price marker of the relevant Sell Goods track one space left for each Goods cube indicated on the tile — just those on the top line if an Outpost, all Goods on the tile if a Colony.

Alternatively, a player whose Fleet(s) occupy a previously-claimed Land may **destroy** an Outpost or Colony, or change a Colony into an Outpost: remove the counter in the first case, flip it in the second case. Move the Price marker of the relevant Sell Goods track one space right for each Goods cube thus lost.

### Run Trade Routes

Each player counts the Trade Gains — Gold and Goods — from Lands where they have Outposts or Colonies, as indicated on each Land tile, and from where they can trace a contiguous line of their Skyships via tile edges or corners to a map square adjacent to Faithdom, and thus a route leading back home: each such line is termed a **Trade Route**. A Trade Route may not be traced over mountain ranges. A Trade Route **may** be traced across the east-west wrap-around. A Trade Route must be complete between a Land and Faithdom for any of that Land's Trade Gains to be counted. Only the top line of Trade Gains (the one not within a broken-line rectangle) may be obtained from a Land with an Outpost: both lines are obtainable from a Land with a Colony. Players who plundered Legends for Gold and Goods, or obtained a Goods cube through the "Licenced Smugglers" Kingdom Advantage, also add these to their total.

### Sell Goods

**All the Goods owned by each player must now be exchanged for Gold**, taken from the bank along with Trade Gains that are Gold. On each Sell Goods track, the number exposed immediately to the right of its Price marker is the current amount of Gold (one, two, three or four) to be received for each of those Goods. Once all selling players have taken their Gold, Price markers are moved back one space right for each Goods cube counted from a Legend or obtained through "Licenced Smugglers".

### Enact Piracy

If a rival Fleet is occupying a map square on a player's Route and no alternative line can be traced, the player pays one Gold to the rival, **unless** the player's Fleet is also present in that square and is declared to protect it. If a rival Fleet sits on multiple Routes, it will take one Gold for each: likewise if there are multiple rival Fleets occupying a Route, then each Fleet takes one Gold for its owner. A player cannot lose more Gold from any one Route than its current value: in the case of multiple rival pirates, priority goes to the Fleet(s) nearest the Land source of the Route. Each player's Fleets may instead **protect** their own Trade Routes in the map squares that they occupy, and may also protect others if the player desires. **A Fleet may engage in Piracy, or in protecting against Piracy, but not both at the same time.** A player with the "Sanctioned Piracy" Kingdom Advantage will also score **1VP for each Route** subject to Piracy by their Fleet(s).

If the **Infidel Fleet** is present face-up in a map square, it has the same effect on all Routes traced through its location as a rival Fleet, except that the Gold paid is returned to the bank.

### Collect Factory Income

From the bank make a pool of Gold equal to the total of Outposts and Colonies. The player with the most Factories (break ties IPO) takes from that pool Gold equal to the number of their Factories, followed by the player with the second-most Factories, and so on until every player has received Gold or the pool is exhausted. If there is still Gold left, repeat the cycle until it is all given out. The last player to receive collects what is left, even if this is less than their number of Factories.

### Elect the Archprelate

Count the votes secured by each player with their Counsellors on Council of Prelates slots, equal to one for each non-player Realm plus the number of Cathedrals for each player Kingdom. If for any reason a Kingdom has no Cathedrals then it has no votes in the Council. If an NPR slot is left vacant, its vote is not counted: if a player Kingdom space is left vacant its votes are presumed to belong to its player. Each player then secretly chooses and simultaneously reveals one of their valid Vote counters, its colour indicating which player is to receive **all their votes** — a player's votes **may not be divided**. Players may agree beforehand to combine their votes in favour of one candidate, with or without reward (see Deals Between Players). A player with the "Patriarch of the Church" Kingdom Advantage always has **one additional vote**.

The player with most votes (or most combined votes) decides which player gets control of the Archprelate: give that player the Archprelate token. Again, the choice of recipient can be the subject of a deal. If there is a tie for most votes, the **incumbent wins** (i.e., the previous Archprelate player retains the token). The victorious player then gains VPs equal to **twice the number of Orthodox Realms divided by three**, rounded down (maximum 6 VPs), and their Heresy track marker is retreated one space.

### Check for an Infidel Invasion

Draw one Infidel Host counter unseen at random from the pool and reveal it. Place it on the Infidel Empire map square beside any others of its kind already drawn. If the tile bears the **"balloon"** symbol it indicates that the square is policed by an **Infidel Fleet** and thus that players' Trade Routes passing through it are subject to Piracy as though by a rival Fleet. If the tile bears the **"attack"** (arrow) symbol it indicates that the entire Infidel Host will invade Faithdom. The players must then raise between them the **Grand Army of the Faith** for collective defence. Heretic Monarchs are **not** excluded from participating.

Set out the Grand Army of the Faith board. The current Archprelate (i.e., the player with the Archprelate token) names one Monarch Captain-General of the Faith and thus nominal commander-in-chief: that player cannot refuse nomination and takes charge of the Captain-General's token. If possible, an Orthodox Monarch must be chosen. Each player IPO decides how many Regiments or Levies to take from their Kingdom and place in their Kingdom's allotted space on the board: then non-player Realms' spaces are each filled with a Grand Army tile, revealed as it is placed. At this point any player who has, or can immediately form, Fleets in their Kingdom, may opt IPO to send one or more Fleets into their allotted space: if there is an Infidel Fleet, the first Fleet(s) to be sent must engage that instead of the ground Host, using the space designated for that purpose. Note which player-contributed forces are largest and second-largest in numbers, and also smallest and second-smallest.

First resolve any aerial combat with the Infidel Fleet (see Battle): the loser retreats to where they came from, the winner's survivors join in the main ground combat. Then resolve the main combat (see Battle). Only the Captain-General player may play Fortunes of War cards from their hand. If the Grand Army's combined strength is greater than the total of hits inflicted on it, then the players have won: except for the Infidel Fleet (which **unless it is totally destroyed** retreats to its Empire), the Host counters go back to their pool. The Captain-General player gains **3VP**; the previously-noted **largest** force gains **5VP** for its player, likewise the second-largest gains **2VP** (**4VP each** if tied). Casualties are taken first by the **largest force (including NPR Grand Army counters)** until it is wiped out, then by the next smallest, and so on until all losses have been absorbed: resolve ties in favour of the earliest IPO (non-player forces always count as later in IPO). Then all Grand Army counters are returned to their pool.

If the Grand Army's strength is less than or equal to the total hits inflicted on it, then the players have lost the battle, and their only remaining recourse is to attempt between them to **buy off** the Infidels. Count the strength of the Host minus the total of hits inflicted on it: this is the quantity of Gold the players must collectively spend to make the Infidels leave. Each player IPO offers a sum of Gold. If the total equals the required quantity, then the Infidel Host disperses as above, and the Gold goes to the bank. Also, the Captain-General player **loses −3VP**; the previously-noted **smallest** force (includes those who contributed nothing) loses **−5VP** for its player, likewise the second-smallest loses **−2VP** (**−4VP** if tied).

If, however, the total money raised is short of the minimum, then the players must make up the difference in further lost VPs. **Each player loses −1VP in order of least to most Gold contributed (break ties IPO) until the difference total is reached** (or all players' VPs are reduced to zero).

**AMENDMENT — Shame of the Grand Army:** If a player contributes neither Regiments, Levies nor Fleets to the Grand Army of the Faith, they are deemed to have shamed their Realm before all of Faithdom. Regardless of the outcome of the battle, the following applies immediately after the Grand Army has been resolved: An Orthodox Monarch who contributed nothing must advance their Heresy track marker one space — the Church publicly condemns their failure to defend the Faith. A Heretic Monarch who contributed nothing must retreat their Heresy track marker one space — their own people condemn them for their cowardice in the face of the common enemy. This shift occurs after all other Heresy track adjustments arising from the invasion have been applied. It cannot push a marker beyond either end of the track.

### Retrieve Fleets

Players may at their option move any or all of their Fleets back to their Kingdom from wherever they are on the map, at no cost, irrespective of distance. This can be done simultaneously, but in the event of who-goes-first conflicts, resolve IPO. Regiments or Levies may be embarked or disembarked at any friendly Lands (i.e., Lands already occupied by that player's Outposts or Colonies) en route.

**Heresy Track VPs:** As per the Heresy track marker position and orientation — purple # (Orthodox) / orange # (Heretic).

**Palaces VP:** The player with the most Palaces scores VPs equal to their number of Palaces minus the 2nd highest number of Palaces: break ties for most IPO.

**Debt Penalty:** Subtract −1VP for every **two** Gold of Debt held by a player.

**Ties for Trade Gains:** Add the affected points together and divide by the number of tied players, rounding up, to decide each player's VP score. For example, if on Round 1 two players are tied for 1st place, add together the points for 1st and 2nd place (3+2 = 5) and divide by 2, giving each player 3 VPs (rounded up). If three or more players tied for 1st place, then all the points would be added together and divided equally. In the case of a tie for 2nd place, add together the points for 2nd and 3rd place and divide by the number of tied players. If there is a tie for 3rd place, only the 3rd place points are divided. No VPs are awarded to players who place 4th or lower.

A player's total of Victory Points can never fall below zero. If a player's VP total exceeds 49 then they receive a "+50 VP" counter and their VP marker commences a second lap of the track: flip the counter to its "+100 VP" side if a third lap starts.

### Final Scoring (Last Round Only)

Players also score VPs as stated on their Legacy cards, amended according to their Monarch's religious alignment at the end of the game: if Orthodox with a purple card, or Heretic with an orange card, they receive all their Legacy VPs; otherwise their Legacy score is **halved** (rounded up).

Players also score 1VP per **five** Gold (rounded down) remaining in their Kingdom.

Resolve final score ties in favour of the player with most leftover Gold, then IPO.

## BATTLE

Battles occur whenever players attack rival players' Fleets (unless the rival evades), the Infidel Fleet, the entire Infidel Host, defended Outposts or Colonies (unless the rival Yields), or attempt to conquer Lands; also if the Infidels invade Faithdom. Except in the last case (for which see "Check for Infidel Invasion"), every battle involves just two players, or one player and a non-player entity.

All fighting consists of a simultaneous exchange of hits ("**Swords**"), those not deflected (by "**Shields**") each causing one Sword loss. Count the Swords and Shields on each side. A side's basic Swords total is also known as its **Strength**, which is its capacity to absorb loss. The totals are modified by the play of one **Fortunes of War** card for each side. If a "No Effect" card is revealed, that side's total is not modified and the discard pile is reshuffled back into the deck. If both sides commit Fleets, those fight each other separately before resolving any combat on the ground.

```
       SIDE A                      SIDE B

  │ + FoW card  │            │ + FoW card  │

             │   EXCHANGE       │
             └────────┬─────────┘

         B's hits on A = B's Swords − A's Shields

  Strength = basic Swords total (before FoW)
  Strength determines capacity to absorb loss.
```

### Fortunes of War Cards

There are 32 cards: two are "No Effect", 15 award one to five Swords, 15 award one to five Shields — in each case three of each value. Swords cards increase a side's hits, **but not its Strength**, which remains the total value of its units' Swords, or the printed Swords of a Land, Contingent or Infidel Host counter.

When played these cards are either drawn from the top of the deck — this is invariably the case for Infidels, Lands and Rebels — or from a player's **hand**. Each player may accumulate a hand of up to **four** Fortunes of War cards: if this maximum is exceeded, the player must discard down to four. If a "No Effect" card is acquired, the player must immediately discard it, reshuffle the discard pile back into the deck, and redraw. Whenever a battle is fought, players with cards in hand may play one or more of these instead of drawing one from the deck. The attacker picks and reveals first, then the defender. With the "Improved Training" Kingdom Advantage, a player **adds one Sword** to the value of each Swords card, and **adds one Shield** to the value of each Shields card, when playing cards from their hand.

### Aerial Battle

In a fight between rival players' Fleets, each Regiment contributes **two Swords**, each Levy contributes **one Sword** and each Skyship contributes **one Sword and one Shield**, to its side. Each side's total Swords minus opposing total Shields, including those contributed by each side's Fortunes of War card, gives the number of hits inflicted: owners apportion losses between their Regiments (which each absorb **two hits**), Levies (which each absorb **one hit**), and Skyships (which each absorb **one hit**). Exception: if the total inflicted is **odd, at least one Skyship or Levy must be lost** — a Regiment or Levy aboard a lost Skyship is also lost unless it can disembark. Victory goes to the Fleet(s) which survived, or else took fewest losses: if losses are equal, the defender wins. The winner compels the losing Fleet(s) to retreat to a neighbouring map square of the winner's choice (or to stay in the same square) and **gains one VP**.

```
  COMBAT VALUES QUICK REFERENCE
  ═════════════════════════════

  UNIT           SWORDS  SHIELDS  ABSORBS
  ─────────────  ──────  ───────  ───────
  Regiment         2       0       2 hits
  Elite Regiment   3       0       2 hits
  Levy             1       0       1 hit
  Skyship          1       1       1 hit
  Fort             0     1/unit*   —

  * 1 Shield per defending Regiment or Levy
    (not those aboard Fleets)
```

### Ground Battle

Regiments defending an Outpost or Colony are each worth **two Swords** and can absorb **two hits**: Levies likewise are each worth **one Sword** and absorb **one hit** (**odd hits are ignored unless Levies are present**). A Fort is worth **one Shield per defending Regiment or Levy**. Calculation and apportioning of losses are as in Aerial Warfare. For the attacker to win, however, the defenders must be **entirely destroyed** (i.e., their losses must equal or exceed their strength). In that case, remove the defender's Skyship from the Outpost or Colony counter and place it elsewhere on the map tile. The Outpost or Colony counter and any Fort remain, uncontrolled: **these cannot be removed** from the map by an attack — this is possible only through a failed attempt to conquer a Land or through an Event. The winner — attacker or defender — **gains one VP**. If an attack fails, surviving attackers must remain where they are until the Retrieve Fleets stage: Regiments or Levies in excess of Skyships are also lost.

Alternatively, prior to resolving combat the attacker may offer the defender the option to **Yield**: if accepted, the defender's Regiments and Levies all go back to their Kingdom, and the Outpost or Colony plus any Fort remains as above but without a fight or a VP gain.

If an Outpost or Colony is undefended (i.e., there are no occupying Regiments or Levies), then the situation is as if it had been Yielded — the former owner's Skyship is removed from the Outpost/Colony counter, there is no fighting and no VP gain.

### Conquest Attempt

This is conducted just like a ground battle, except that the attacker can add their Garrison Regiments and Levies (**but not Fort Shields**) to their force: the Land defends with its printed Swords and Shields, never Yields, and always has a Fortunes of War card drawn for it from the deck. To win the attacker must inflict hits equal to or exceeding the Strength of the Land, and some part of the attacking Fleet(s) must survive the combat. If successful, the attacker gains **1VP** and places a Colony counter on the Land (or flips the existing Outpost counter to its Colony side). In the event of failure, any Outpost or Fort is lost (i.e., removed to their supply), as are any Regiments or Levies that cannot fit aboard the surviving Fleet Skyships. Surviving attackers and any Trade Route Skyship remain in the map square.

### Optional Rule: Tactical Alliances

If three or more players' Fleets contest the same map square, it might be advantageous for some of those players to temporarily ally against the others. Be aware that employing this rule may considerably lengthen the game through players halting to negotiate the terms of their alliance. If this occurs, for the duration of the Encounter Stage section in which the agreement holds the allied Fleets do not count as rivals (and so could conduct a ground battle or conquest attempt together).

In the case of an aerial battle, the allies at the outset combine their fleets as if they belonged to the same player, but before any Fortunes of War cards are played they must:

1. Decide which of them will take overall command — that player decides whether to fight or evade, their position IPO determines when they can challenge, and their hand of FoW cards (if any) are the ones they may use, or else draw from the deck if they prefer or must.
2. Decide how to allocate their losses — if this is **not** agreed before combat, then the biggest of the Fleets will take all losses until it is wiped out, then the 2nd biggest and so on until all losses are satisfied, ties broken IPO.
3. If fighting over a Legend, agree how to allocate the plunder rewards.

In the case of a ground battle or conquest attempt, the allies must additionally agree which of them Claims the Land (or that none of them do): it cannot be shared. Such arrangements are considered binding for each contested map square where they are made, for the current Encounter Stage section.

## HERESY TRACKS

Each Kingdom's Heresy track represents the rising religious conflict as the mismatch between the new discoveries and Church dogma grows ever wider, coupled with existing discontent over prelate corruption, and its effect on each Monarch's popularity. Each Track consists of 19 spaces: each Heresy track marker starts on the first space, and cannot advance beyond the 19th or retreat beyond the first. The position of their Heresy track marker and their Monarch's religious allegiance determines how many VPs each player scores from their Heresy track each Scoring Phase.

```
  HERESY TRACK (19 spaces)
  ════════════════════════

  Orthodox                                         Heretic
  (purple)                                         (orange)
  ◄─────────────────────────────────────────────────────►
  ┌──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┬──┐

  ↑ Start                        Each space has two VP values:
                                  Purple (Orthodox) / Orange (Heretic)
```

### Summary of Heresy Track Movements

```
  HERESY TRACK — WHAT MOVES IT
  ═════════════════════════════

  ADVANCES MARKER (→)                 RETREATS MARKER (←)
  ─────────────────────               ──────────────────────
  Discover Legend/new Race (all)      Found Cathedral (−1)
  Claim Land (+1)                     Elected Archprelate (−1)
  Plunder Legend (+1)                 Reform Dogma (all, −1)
  Confirm Dogma (all, +1)            Orthodox wins vs Heretic (−1)
  Heretic wins vs Heretic (−1)       Punish Heretics (Orthodox)
  Punish Believers (Heretic)          Found Palace (player choice: ±1)
  Found Palace (player choice: ±1)
  Shame (Orthodox, no contribution
   to Grand Army: +1)

  RETREATS (←) specific:
  Shame (Heretic, no contribution
   to Grand Army: −1)
```

**Effect of Discoveries** — Each time a Legend tile or a Land belonging to a previously-undiscovered Race is revealed, **all players'** Heresy track markers are advanced one space.

**Effect of Claiming Lands or Plundering Legends** — Each time a Land is claimed (even if from a previous owner or for a second or subsequent time) or a Legend plundered, the responsible player's Heresy track marker is advanced one space.

**Effect of New Cathedrals/Palaces** — Each time a Cathedral is founded, the owning player's Heresy track marker is retreated one space. Each time a Palace is founded, the owning player's Heresy track marker is advanced **or** retreated one space, **as that player chooses**.

**Effect of Punishing Dissenters** — Each Dissenter put in the Prison space (imprisoned) causes its Kingdom's Heresy track marker to be retreated one space if the Monarch is Orthodox, advanced one space if the Monarch is Heretic. Each Dissenter returned to its pool from the Prison space (executed) causes the same **and the player to lose −1VP**. A maximum of **three** Dissenters can be punished in one action, and no more than three can be held in Prison at one time: this rises to **four** Dissenters with the "More Prisons" Kingdom Advantage.

**Effect of Conversion** — If a player flips their Heresy track marker to Heretic (orange) side up, at the subsequent scoring they count the orange-highlighted VPs under the marker: if vice versa, they count the purple-highlighted VPs. At the instant of conversion, all Dissenters in the Prison space must be returned to their pool (released): each Dissenter thus released causes the Heresy track marker to be advanced one space if the conversion is to Heresy, or to be retreated one space if the conversion is to Orthodoxy.

**Effect of Holy Decrees to Confirm or Reform Dogma** — If the Archprelate player decides to Confirm Dogma, **all players'** Heresy track markers are advanced one space. If to Reform Dogma, all players' Heresy track markers are retreated one space.

**Effect of Archprelate Election** — The winning player's Heresy track marker is retreated one space.

**Effect of Orthodox vs Heretic Monarchy Battle Victories** — An Orthodox winner's Heresy track marker is retreated one space; a Heretic winner's Heresy track marker is advanced one space.

## ANYTIME OPTIONS

The following can be exercised by a player at any time. If there is a conflict over priority with other players, resolve IPO.

1. Sell Skyships in your Kingdom to the bank for one Gold per Skyship, or to another player for an agreed price. Sold Skyships go back to their supply; the other player (if involved) draws an equal number of Skyships from theirs and adds them to their Kingdom board or to a Fleet in their Kingdom.
2. Transfer Outposts or Colonies to another player whose Skyship or Fleet is present for an agreed price. Garrisons are returned to their Kingdom.
3. Sell a Cathedral or Palace to the bank for three Gold. Move the relevant marker one space downward (or off board if reduced to zero). **Players cannot sell their last Palace; only Heretic Monarchs can sell Cathedrals.**
4. Move Skyships, Regiments and Levies between your Kingdom board unassigned area and Fleets in your Kingdom.

## DEALS BETWEEN PLAYERS

Players are free to make deals at any time, as long as these are made verbally while seated at the table — no secret messages or discussions permitted unless all players agree to allow such, being aware that this could significantly lengthen playing time.

Players may agree to exchange Gold, Outposts/Colonies (including Forts), Skyships located in their Kingdom, Council of Prelates votes, award of the Archprelate token and promises. Players may **not** exchange Regiments, Levies, Skyships located outside their Kingdom, buildings in their Kingdom or VPs. For an Outpost or Colony to be transferred the recipient must have a Skyship (or a Fleet) in the same map square.

Deals involving an immediate exchange must be honoured. Promises of some action or item at a later time can be honoured or not as players wish.

## SETTING UP A GAME

Place the Known World tile somewhere slightly off-centre on the table, and the Action board at one end of the table, about 12" from the Known World. Place stars of each player's colour on the **10** space of the Victory Points track, and place the Current Round marker (gold cylinder) on the "1" space of the Round track. Place the Captain-General of the Faith token and the Grand Army of the Faith board somewhere in reach. If there are fewer than six players, place Counsellor cylinders of the unused colours on the rightmost slots for Player Order to show these are not in use.

### Map Tile Layout

Shuffle the map tiles and lay them face-down unseen around the Known World according to the following pattern:

```
  STANDARD MAP LAYOUT (8 x 4)
  ════════════════════════════

  ┌───┬───┬───┬═══╤═══┬───┬───┬───┐
  │ M │ M │ M ║ K │ K ║ M │ M │ M │
  ├───┼───┼───╠═══╪═══╬───┼───┼───┤
  │ M │ M │ M ║ K │ K ║ M │ M │ M │
  ├───┼───┼───╬───┼───╬───┼───┼───┤
  │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │
  ├───┼───┼───┼───┼───┼───┼───┼───┤
  │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │
  └───┴───┴───┴───┴───┴───┴───┴───┘

  M = Map Tile space (face down)
  K = Known World (4 map squares: Faithdom,
      Infidel Empire, 2x Ocean Sea)
```

Make a bank of all the Gold cubes and discs. Make separate pools of all Dissenter cubes, Heretic non-player Realm discs, Rebels counters, Grand Army counters, Infidel Host counters, Fort models and Outpost/Colony counters. The Rebels, Grand Army and Infidel Host counters must be face-down and randomised. Place a Price marker cube on the rightmost space of each Sell Goods track, each matching the cube symbol at the track's head.

Each player takes and places in front of them the Kingdom board in their colour. Beside their board each player makes pools of Skyships (discs), Regiments (cubes), Levies (grey cubes) and Counsellors (cylinders) in their colour. In the central space of their Kingdom board each player places six Gold, three Skyships, six Regiments, four Counsellors and up to six Archprelate Vote tokens (one for each player colour in the game); also one Factories, one Palaces and one Cathedrals marker on the bottommost ("1") space of the Grand Buildings track, and their Heresy track marker on the leftmost "starred" space of the Heresy track, purple (i.e., Orthodox) side up. In each Fleet box the players place each of their Fleet models on the space matching their base shape (square, hexagon, circle) — **this signifies then and subsequently that a Fleet is located in its Kingdom**.

Set the initial player order as clockwise around the table, starting from the player who last flew (or ruled a kingdom): place one Player Order marker for each from their supply on the Player Order track accordingly. The **last** player on the track becomes the first Archprelate, and takes the Archprelate token. Then each player in **reverse** player order chooses one Kingdom Advantage card (from a deck of seven) to apply to their Kingdom alone throughout the game. A player who selected the "Elite Regiments" Kingdom Advantage immediately replaces three of their Regiments (returned to their pool) with three Elite Regiments (purple cubes).

Shuffle the Legacy cards and deal three to each player, each of whom picks one and places it face-down by their Kingdom board, discarding the others without revealing them. Shuffle the (remaining) Event cards and deal three to each player. Shuffle the Fortunes of War cards. Place both decks somewhere in reach.

## VARIANTS

The following variants can be played singly or in any combination.

### Short Game

Play for four Rounds instead of six: conduct final scoring at the end of the fourth Round. At the start, remove from the game four of the twelve Infidel Host counters — one each of 30 Swords and 15 Swords, plus two each of 10 Swords.

### Variable Length Game

Play until the end of a Round in which the last map tile is placed: conduct final scoring on that round.

### Smaller World

Recommended for games with greater interaction, or with fewer than four players. The world-space is 6×3 instead of 8×4:

```
  SMALLER WORLD LAYOUT (6 x 3)
  ═════════════════════════════

  ┌───┬───┬═══╤═══┬───┬───┐
  │ M │ M ║ K │ K ║ M │ M │
  ├───┼───╠═══╪═══╬───┼───┤
  │ M │ M ║ K │ K ║ M │ M │
  ├───┼───╬───┼───╬───┼───┤
  │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │ M │
  └───┴───┴───┴───┴───┴───┘
```

Separate the map tiles into Lands, Legends and Oceans: further separate the Lands into the six fantasy races (six stacks of three). Invert face-down and shuffle each of the Lands stacks, then pick the top one from each. Combine the remaining 12 Lands, shuffle and pick the top three. Invert and shuffle all the Legends and pick the top three. Pick two Ocean tiles. Combine and invert all 14 picked tiles and shuffle them, then lay them out as in the diagram above. Otherwise set up the game as normal. When dispatched, Fleets move a maximum of **three** map squares per Round. **Double** all marker shifts on the Heresy track for discovering Legends (i.e., advance two spaces instead of one). If "Merchant" Legacy cards are chosen, they are worth **2VP per Trade Gain** instead of 1VP.

### Peaceful Game

One where the players do not fight each other. Remove the "Peace Accord Reached" and "Dynastic Alliance" cards from the Event Card deck prior to shuffling and dealing. Then play as normal, except that no player may attack another nor seize their Outposts or Colonies: they can, however, conquer Lands, engage in Piracy, play Events to another player's disadvantage and support Rebellions.

### More Eventful Game

Draw **two** Event cards per Events Phase instead of one: resolve the effects of the first card drawn before the second, unless the first is a Rebellion or Encounter and the second is not.

### Uneventful Game

Ignore the Events Phase and Event Cards completely.

### Controlled Discoveries Game

Instead of laying a face-down array at the start, make a stack of map tiles, draw the top three and lay them face-up. In the Discoveries Phase, each player IPO picks one of these and places it, then draws a new tile from the stack: if the placed tile is a Legend or Ocean, then the same player picks another and places it next to the previous tile. The array boundaries still apply. If also using the Smaller World variant, follow the latter's instructions to make a 14-tile stack.

## GLOSSARY OF TERMS

**Action** — Anything selected to be done in the Actions Phase.

**Action Board** — The board, common to all players, bearing the VP track (around the edge), Round rondel and the tracks and slots for: Player Order, Gather Taxes, Appoint Counsellors, Recruit Regiments, Purchase Skyships, Found Factories, Found Cathedrals, Found Palaces, Found Shipyards, Found Forts, Issue Holy Decree and Influence Prelates.

**Archprelate** — The Church of the Faith's elected head: the player who controls the Archprelate may gain VPs for holding the office, issue Holy Decrees and, in the event of Infidel invasion, choose a Monarch to be Captain-General of the Grand Army of the Faith.

**Attacker** — The side initiating combat: counts as attacking even if no battle occurs.

**Battle** — The act of engaging in combat: the section of the rules governing the various kinds of combat.

**Blessing** — A Holy Decree: an honour with VPs conferrable on a Monarch who is Orthodox and whose Heresy track marker is least advanced.

**Captain-General** — Of the Faith. An honour temporarily conferred on one player by the Archprelate in the event of Infidel invasion: this player presides over the battle and stands to gain or lose VPs depending on the outcome.

**Cathedrals** — Represented by a purple building marker on the Grand Buildings track: each new one scores 2VPs for its player, retreats their Heresy track marker one space and adds one vote to their Kingdom's Council of Prelates delegation for the Archprelate election.

**Church of the Faith** — The religious order which initially unites Faithdom in Orthodox belief.

**Colony** — A Land of Faerie which has been claimed and conquered, making all of its Trade Gains available to its possessor: signified by the Outpost/Colony counter placed "Colony" side up.

**Confirm Dogma** — A Holy Decree by which the Church persists in its pre-discoveries worldview: this has the effect of advancing all Heresy track markers.

**Council of Prelates** — The body whose votes periodically elect the Archprelate: the device through which the players seek control of the Archprelate's powers.

**Counsellors** — Agents through which the Monarch enacts decisions: cylinders in player colours used to mark selected Actions.

**Curse** — A Holy Decree applicable against a Monarch who has turned Heretic or who, while Orthodox, presides over the most advanced Heresy track marker. Subtracts VPs from the victim.

**Debt** — Gold spent in excess of the quantity available to a player: the space on the Kingdom board in which it is recorded.

**Defender** — The side subjected to combat by an attacker.

**Disembarkation** — Regiments or Levies leaving a Fleet: this is not possible if the Fleet is in a Legend or Ocean tile, or a Land tile which is unclaimed, or defended by a rival player's Fleet or Garrison.

**Dissenters** — Heretics in an Orthodox Kingdom, believers in a Heretic Kingdom (plain wood cubes): up to three Dissenters can be imprisoned, or executed if already imprisoned, in one action.

**Event Cards** — Cards detailing random happenings affecting either all or certain players, drawn to be enacted each Round.

**Factories** — Represented by a grey building marker on the Grand Buildings track: each gains one or more Gold per Round from a pool equal to the total number of Outposts and Colonies, the Kingdom with most getting the first draw, then the second most, and so on until the pool is emptied.

**Faerie** — Everywhere beyond the Known World: the collective noun for all the Land, Legend and Ocean map tiles.

**Faithdom** — The collective noun for the nine named Realms: the starting map square for the game's protagonists.

**Fleets** — Collections of up to five Skyships placed in a Fleet box: each Skyship may be partnered with a Regiment or Levy.

**Fleet Boxes** — Repositories of Skyships and Regiments assigned to Fleets.

**Fleet Models** — Miniatures in player colours resembling galleons hoisted by balloons, each representing a Fleet and its movement, and each distinguished by a base-shape (square, hexagon or circle) which matches that on its Fleet box.

**Forts** — Defensive structures which may be built, one to a Land or Kingdom, provided there is at least one Regiment or Levy in that location: Forts grant one Shield per Regiment or Levy in a Land or Kingdom.

**Fortunes of War** — Cards. A chance effect in combat: play one card from the deck per side, or one or more if played from a hand of up to four, adding a number of Swords or Shields to the resolution, or else "no effect" and requiring the discard pile to be returned to the deck and reshuffled.

**FoW** — Fortunes of War.

**Garrisons** — One or more Regiments or Levies present in Lands to defend Outposts and Colonies.

**Gold** — The currency of the game, in ones (gold cubes) and fives (gold discs).

**Goods** — Exotic items obtainable only from Faerie, worth a variable amount of Gold: these are Dragonscales (turquoise cubes), Krakenskin (khaki cubes), Magic Dust (pink cubes), Mithril (silver cubes), Sticky Ichor (sky blue cubes) and Pipeweed (brown cubes).

**Grand Army** — Counters representing the military strength committed by NPRs to defence against an Infidel invasion, chosen at random.

**Grand Army of the Faith** — A body for collective defence, to which players and NPRs are summoned to contribute military forces in the event of an Infidel invasion.

**Grand Buildings** — The collective term for the Cathedrals, Palaces and Factories a player may found: the Grand Buildings Track on each Kingdom board holds these and the starting allocation of one of each.

**Heresy track** — A 19-square track along the sides and top of each Kingdom board, each square marked with two VP values, one (orange) that applies if the Monarch is a Heretic, the other (purple) if Orthodox: the Heresy track marker, flippable to show the Monarch's current religious allegiance, advances with discoveries, claims, plundering and punishing Believers, retreats with building Cathedrals, being voted Archprelate and punishing Heretics, moves either way with building Palaces, winning Orthodox vs Heretic battles, some Holy Decrees and certain Events. Each Scoring Phase each player receives points according to the position of their marker and religious allegiance.

**Holy Decree** — An edict issued by an Archprelate: the Archprelate player may order one decree per Round out of a list — curse a Monarch, bless a Monarch, confirm or reform Church dogma (affecting all Realms). The choice of Monarch is restricted.

**Infidel Empire** — The map square just south of Faithdom: a hostile non-player state, too powerful to be conquered, liable to mount invasions. It does NOT count as a Land, so cannot play host to an Outpost or Colony.

**Infidel Fleet** — An Infidel Host counter representing the Infidel Empire's acquisition of Skyships, available to invade Faithdom but also used to defend Infidel airspace and conduct Piracy against Trade Routes running through it: once picked it will act aggressively against intruding Fleets.

**Infidel Host** — A counter representing a force of Infidels. These are picked at random to accumulate in the Infidel Empire at one per Round, available to invade Faithdom: this occurs when a Host counter with the "attack" (arrow) symbol (one of two) is picked.

**IPO** — In Player Order.

**Kingdom** — One of six Realms ruled by a Monarch, any of which can be adopted by a player.

**Kingdom Advantage** — Any of seven cards from which players each choose one at the start of a game, each conferring some special rule to its player's benefit.

**Kingdom Board** — The mat on which each player displays their Gold (or Gold Debt), Goods (prior to sale), Fort (if any), unassigned Counsellors, Skyships, Regiments and Levies in their Kingdom, Grand Buildings, and imprisoned Dissenters: also contains their Heresy track and marker, Fleet Boxes with assigned Skyships and Regiments or Levies, and the Build Skyships, Punish Dissenters, Convert Monarch, Conscript Levies, Train Troops and Dispatch 1st, 2nd and 3rd Fleet action slots.

**Known World** — The large starting tile in each game, containing four map squares — Faithdom, the Infidel Empire and the two-square Ocean Sea: the world as it was known to the protagonists prior to the first discoveries.

**Land** — A map tile named for one of six races, who can be traded with or conquered: each tile bears its defence capability (Swords and Shields) and two rows of Trade Gains, the top row obtainable by planting an Outpost, the lower row in addition only through conquest, converting the Outpost into a Colony.

**Legacy Cards** — These represent monarchs' initially secret ambitions for their reign: a source of additional VPs dependent on some metric of achievement. Each player secretly chooses one card out of three, to reveal and score at the game's end. Some Events may compel a mid-game scoring and change of card.

**Legend** — A map tile, containing either sea or a desert land, named for a legendary place, monster or people, bearing an image of the named thing and a bonus of Trade Gains or VPs, obtained by players whose Fleets visit.

**Levies** — Bodies of inferior soldiers raised by conscription, represented by grey cubes. In combat each Levy is worth one Sword, and absorbs one hit.

**Map Square** — A distinct region of the map, including those of the Known World.

**Map Tile** — A square tile bearing a Land, Legend or Ocean of Faerie, which becomes a visitable region of the map upon Discovery.

**Monarch** — The ruler of a Kingdom of Faithdom, represented by a player.

**Mountain Ranges** — Triangular peak symbols populating edges or corners of some map squares: these block laden Fleet movement and tracing of Trade Routes through those edges and corners.

**NPR** — Non-player Realm: the Prelacy, the Republics (Venoa and Zeeland) and any Kingdom not represented by a player.

**Ocean** — A map square containing only sea (and images of fish etc).

**Outpost** — A Land of Faerie which has been claimed, signified by the placing of an Outpost/Colony counter "Outpost" side up, with the claiming player's Skyship disc placed on top.

**Palaces** — Represented by a gold building marker on the Grand Buildings track: each new one scores 1VP for an Orthodox Monarch, 2VP for a Heretic Monarch, and advances or retreats that Kingdom's Heresy track marker one space according to the player's choice. Each Scoring Phase the player with most Palaces scores VPs equal to the difference between their number of Palaces and that of the player with second most Palaces.

**Peace of the Faith** — A non-aggression pact between the Realms agreed before the discovery of Faerie, which strictly applies only to home territory.

**Piracy** — After the Sell Goods stage, the payment of Gold to players whose Fleets occupy map squares through which rivals' Trade Routes pass, unless faced off by defending Fleets. The Infidel Fleet, if in play, will also cause Gold to be paid (to the bank) from Routes through the Infidel Empire.

**Player Order (PO)** — The order in which players select their actions, which changes in the course of the game: recorded by the position of PO markers on the Player Order Track.

**Prelacy** — The central realm of Faithdom: the seat of the Church of the Faith, the home of the Archprelate and where the Council of Prelates meets.

**Price** — The current value in Gold of each of the six types of Goods, varying by quantity in Trade Routes, shown by a cube marker on each Sell Goods track.

**Prison** — The space on the Kingdom board into which up to three Dissenters at a time are placed to be punished.

**Race** — The type of creature (Dwarves, Elves, Goblins, Halflings, Orcs or Trolls) inhabiting a Land.

**Realm** — Any one part of Faithdom, signified by its name and distinct colour.

**Rebels** — Counters representing military forces raised by an Event against a player Kingdom from within, chosen at random.

**Rebellion** — Event cards bearing this term instigate a Battle internal to a Kingdom, invoking common special rules governing the rebel force and intervention by other players (exception: "Colonial Rebellion").

**Reform Dogma** — A Holy Decree by which the Church somewhat revises its worldview in line with discoveries: this has the effect of retreating all Heresy track markers.

**Regiments** — Bodies of soldiers, represented by cubes in their Kingdom colour. In combat each Regiment is worth two Swords, and thus absorbs two hits.

**Republics** — Two Non-Player Realms, Venoa and Zeeland, which are neutral towards player rivalries: their main part in the game is as the notional site of Shipyards from which any player can purchase Skyships.

**Resolution Board** — A reminder of the Resolution phase sequence and tracks with Goods cube markers showing the current value in Gold of each of the six types of Goods.

**Round** — A complete sequence of phases: an extended game consists of six Rounds, a short game of four Rounds, a variable length game of the number of Rounds taken to reveal all the map tiles.

**Shields** — Defensive combat power: each Shield cancels one opposing Sword.

**Shipyard Tiles** — Places in a Kingdom for building Skyships: each can build up to two Skyships in a Round. During a game players can found up to three Shipyards in their Kingdom. Place active Shipyard counters on or beside their Kingdom board, "Skyship" side up.

**Skyships** — Large aerial vessels, represented by discs in their Kingdom colour: deployed singly in map squares linking Lands to Kingdoms they act as Trade Routes; placed on Fleet boxes with or without Regiments or Levies they form Fleets to engage in Battle, Claiming, Plundering or Piracy. In combat each Skyship is worth one Shield and one Sword, and thus absorbs one loss. Each Skyship can carry one Regiment or Levy.

**Strength** — Capacity to absorb losses: the total of Swords in a force, or printed on a counter or on a Land tile, not counting additional Swords granted by a Fortunes of War card.

**Swords** — Offensive combat power: each Sword not cancelled by a Shield causes one hit against an opposing force.

**Taxes** — Gold obtained each Action Phase by the players from within their Kingdoms.

**Trade Gains** — The number of Gold or Goods cubes obtained or obtainable from a Land or Legend: the total of such held by a player.

**Trade Route** — A line of one or more individual Skyships of a player's colour in adjacent map squares, traced via non-mountainous edges and/or corners, linking a player's Outpost or Colony to Faithdom: the first such Skyship must be in the Outpost/Colony (and serves to mark ownership) but none is placed in the Faithdom map square.

**Turn** — A period of the Actions Phase in which a player selects one Action to perform.

**VP** — Victory Point: VPs are recorded on the Victory Point track by a star marker for each player.

**Yield** — Submit prior to combat. In the case of an Outpost/Colony under attack, the attacker may offer the defender the option to Yield: if accepted, the defending Regiments or Levies go back to their Kingdom via either party's Trade Route and the Outpost/Colony changes ownership. In the case of a Rebellion Event, the defender may choose to Yield and undergo whatever Event outcome is mandated by an attacker victory but without fighting.
