# EMPIRES OF THE SKY

## Amendments to Rulebook v4.2 — Manufactured Rivalry Expansion

---

These amendments modify and extend the base rules of Empires of the Sky v4.2 to improve strategic balance, eliminate degenerate strategies, and ensure meaningful player interaction from round 1. They are organised by the phase or system they affect.

---

## AMENDMENT — SETTING UP A GAME

### Kingdom Advantage Card Draft

Replace the paragraph beginning "Then each player in **reverse** player order chooses one Kingdom Advantage card" with the following:

**AMENDMENT — Rivalry-Paired Kingdom Advantage Draft:** The 7 Kingdom Advantage cards are organised into three rivalry pairs plus one wild card:

- **Group A (Economic):** Licenced Smugglers ↔ Sanctioned Piracy
- **Group B (Military):** Elite Regiments ↔ Improved Training
- **Group C (Religious):** Patriarch of the Church ↔ More Prisons
- **Wild Card:** More Efficient Taxation

When fewer than 7 cards are needed, remove cards according to the following rules. A rivalry pair must never be broken — if one card of a pair is in the pool, the other must also be present.

**6 players:** All 7 cards used. No change.

**5 players (6 cards):** Remove only More Efficient Taxation. All three rivalry pairs remain intact.

**4 players (5 cards):** Remove exactly 2 cards from **different** rivalry groups. When removing one card from a rivalry pair, remove the **weaker** card — i.e., the one that provides less standalone value. More Efficient Taxation may be one of the 2 removed. At least 2 complete rivalry pairs must remain.

**3 players (4 cards):** Keep 2 complete rivalry pairs (4 cards). Remove the third pair and More Efficient Taxation. The choice of which 2 pairs to keep determines game flavour: Groups A + B favours trade wars and conquest; Groups A + C favours trade empires and church politics; Groups B + C favours military and religious conflict.

**2 players (3 cards):** Keep 1 complete rivalry pair plus More Efficient Taxation. Each player picks 1, leaving 1 unused.

After the pool is constructed, selection proceeds in reverse player order as normal. Players can see the full pool before choosing.

### Legacy Card Seeded Deal

Replace the paragraph beginning "Shuffle the Legacy cards and deal three to each player" with the following:

**AMENDMENT — Seeded Legacy Card Deal:** Legacy cards are dealt using a structured pool system that guarantees archetype diversity, rivalry presence, and colour (alignment) diversity in each player's hand.

**Step 1 — Sort cards into pools, preserving colour.** Separate the 18 Legacy cards into three pools. Each card retains its printed colour (purple for Orthodox, orange for Heretic).

- **Pool α (Colonial):** The Conqueror ×2, The Navigator ×2, The Merchant ×2 (6 cards — each type has one purple and one orange copy)
- **Pool β (Religious):** The Pious ×2, The Magnificent ×2 (4 cards — both Pious are purple, both Magnificent are orange)
- **Pool γ (Generalist):** The Great ×2, The Builder ×2, The Aviator ×2, The Mighty ×2 (8 cards — each type has one purple and one orange copy)

Shuffle each pool independently.

**Step 2 — Deal one card per player from each pool.** For each player in random order, deal 1 card from Pool α, 1 from Pool β, and 1 from Pool γ. Every player now holds 3 cards from 3 different archetypes.

**Step 3 — Rivalry presence check.** Verify the following across all dealt hands. If a check fails, swap one card in another player's hand for the required card from the undealt remainder.

- If any player holds The Conqueror, at least one other player must hold The Navigator or The Mighty.
- If any player holds The Pious, at least one other player must hold The Magnificent, and vice versa.
- If any player holds The Builder, at least one other player must hold The Great, and vice versa.

**Step 4 — Cross-system synergy check.** Each player's Kingdom Advantage is already locked from the draft. Check whether any player's locked Kingdom Advantage combined with a card in their legacy hand forms a particularly dominant combination ("nuclear combo"). If so, verify that the corresponding counter-combination is available in at least one other player's hand. If not, swap one legacy card in the counter-player's hand to include the required counter card. Only legacy cards are swapped — never Kingdom Advantages. Swap at most 1 card.

The nuclear combinations and their counters are:

| Nuclear Combo | Counter That Must Exist |
|---|---|
| Licenced Smugglers + The Merchant | Sanctioned Piracy + The Mighty |
| Improved Training + The Conqueror | Elite Regiments + The Mighty |
| More Efficient Taxation + The Great | Sanctioned Piracy + The Navigator |
| Patriarch of the Church + The Pious | More Prisons + The Magnificent |

**Step 5 — Colour diversity check (soft).** If all three cards in a player's hand are the same colour (all purple or all orange), swap the Pool α or Pool γ card for the **opposite-colour copy of the same card type** from the undealt remainder. If no opposite-colour copy is available, leave the hand as-is. This check runs last to avoid conflicting with earlier swaps.

**Step 6 — Shuffle within hands.** Randomise the order of each player's 3 cards. Each player then picks one and places it face-down by their Kingdom board, discarding the others without revealing them.

## AMENDMENT — PHASE 1: EVENTS

### New Event Cards

The following Event cards are added to the game. Their deck assignment (Early or Late) is noted above.

**Guild Revolt** — *Factory Workers Demand Better Conditions.* Target: the player with the most Factories (break ties IPO). Effect: that player must either pay **2 Gold per Factory** they own to the bank, or sell **one Factory** to the bank for 3 Gold. If the player cannot pay and has only one Factory, they pay what they can.

**Corruption Scandal** — *Cathedral Prelates Caught in Embezzlement.* Target: the player with the most Cathedrals (break ties IPO). Effect: lose **one Cathedral** (sold to bank for 3 Gold) OR lose **3 VP**. Player's choice. A player with only one Cathedral must choose the VP penalty.

**Royal Patronage** — *The Crown Rewards Swift Exploration.* Target: all players. Effect: the first player to claim a Land this Round gains **+2 VP** and **2 Gold** from the bank. If no Land is claimed this Round, the event has no effect.

**Race to Discovery** — *Rival Courts Compete for Prestige.* Target: all players. Effect: the player who discovers the most tiles this Round (including tiles revealed by chain-flipping) gains **+1 VP per tile** discovered beyond the number discovered by the player with the second-most discoveries. Break ties IPO. If no tiles are discovered, the event has no effect.

**Foreign Agitators** — *Neighbouring Realms Send Troublemakers.* Target: all players. Effect: each player places **one Dissenter cube** in the Kingdom of the player next to them in Player Order (1st places in 2nd's Kingdom, 2nd in 3rd's, and so on; the last player places in 1st's Kingdom). These Dissenters are not imprisoned — they are placed in the Kingdom's central area. They must be dealt with during that player's next Punish Dissenters action, or each unimprisoned Dissenter will shift their Heresy track marker by one space at the next Scoring Phase (in the direction appropriate to that Monarch's alignment: advance if Orthodox, retreat if Heretic).

## AMENDMENT — PHASE 5: RESOLUTION

### Collect Factory Income — Engaged Factories Rule

Add the following after the existing Collect Factory Income rules:

**AMENDMENT — Engaged Factories:** During Collect Factory Income, a player's effective Factory count is **capped at their number of active Trade Routes**. A Trade Route is "active" if it connects an Outpost or Colony to Faithdom via a complete, unbroken chain of that player's Skyships. A player with 6 Factories but only 2 active Trade Routes collects Factory income as if they had 2 Factories. A player with zero active Trade Routes collects no Factory income regardless of their Factory count.

This rule applies only to the collection of Factory income. It does not affect the founding of Factories, the Factory building marker position, or any other game mechanic that references a player's number of Factories.

### Enact Piracy — Cut the Route Option

Add the following after the existing Enact Piracy rules:

**AMENDMENT — Cut the Route:** During Enact Piracy, instead of collecting piracy Gold from a Trade Route passing through their Fleet's map square, a player may choose to **cut** the route: remove **one** rival Trade Route Skyship from that map square and return it to the rival's Kingdom. The cutting player gains **no Gold** from that route this Round. The cutting player's Fleet is considered to have engaged in Piracy for purposes of the "A Fleet may engage in Piracy, or in protecting against Piracy, but not both at the same time" rule.

A player may choose to cut or to collect for each separate route that passes through their Fleet's square — they need not make the same choice for all routes.

If a player has the "Sanctioned Piracy" Kingdom Advantage, cutting a route does **not** score 1 VP — the VP is only awarded when Gold is collected through piracy, not when a route is cut. The Sanctioned Piracy player must choose between the VP (by collecting Gold) and the strategic disruption (by cutting).

### Elect the Archprelate — Archprelate Fatigue (Optional)

Add the following after the existing Elect the Archprelate rules:

**AMENDMENT — Archprelate Fatigue (Optional Rule):** If the same player wins the Archprelate election in **consecutive** Rounds, their VP reward is reduced by **2** (minimum 0) for each consecutive win after the first.

- First win: full VP reward (as per standard rules).
- Second consecutive win: VP reward minus 2.
- Third consecutive win: VP reward minus 4.
- And so on.

The Heresy track marker retreat of one space upon winning still applies regardless of the VP reduction. The counter resets to zero when a different player wins the election. If the incumbent wins by tie, this still counts as a consecutive win.

## AMENDMENT — ANYTIME OPTIONS

### Send Agitators (Optional)

Add the following to the list of Anytime Options:

**5. Send Agitators (Optional Rule)** — A player may pay **2 Gold** to the bank to place one Dissenter cube directly into a rival's Kingdom (not imprisoned — placed in the Kingdom's central area). That rival must deal with the Dissenter during their next Punish Dissenters action. If the Dissenter remains unimprisoned at the next Scoring Phase, it shifts the rival's Heresy track marker by one space (advance if the rival is Orthodox, retreat if Heretic). After the shift, the Dissenter is returned to its pool.

A player may send at most **one** Agitator per rival per Round. Agitators count toward Prison capacity if imprisoned. The "More Prisons" Kingdom Advantage provides an additional Prison space for handling Agitators alongside regular Dissenters.

## AMENDMENT — ISSUE HOLY DECREE

### Inquisition Decree (Optional)

Add the following as a fifth option to the Issue Holy Decree action:

**5. Inquisition (Optional Rule)** — The Archprelate targets one Kingdom that has **at least one imprisoned Dissenter**. That Kingdom's player must immediately **release all imprisoned Dissenters** (return cubes to their pool) and **advance** their Heresy track marker by **2 spaces**. The releasing of Dissenters does **not** cause additional Heresy track shifts beyond the 2-space advance mandated by the Inquisition — i.e., the normal reversal effect of releasing prisoners is replaced, not stacked.

The Archprelate player must pay **2 Gold** to the bank to issue an Inquisition decree. A player may not Inquisition their own Kingdom.

## GLOSSARY ADDITIONS

**Active Trade Route** — A Trade Route connecting an Outpost or Colony to Faithdom via a complete, unbroken chain of a player's Skyships. Used to determine effective Factory count under the Engaged Factories rule.

**Agitator** — A Dissenter cube placed in a rival's Kingdom through the Send Agitators anytime option or the Foreign Agitators event. Functions as a normal Dissenter for imprisonment and execution purposes.

**Combined Deck** — The single Event card deck formed at the start of Round 3 by merging the Early and Late decks.

**Early Deck** — The sub-deck of Event cards viable at game start, used exclusively during Rounds 1 and 2.

**Epoch-Based Deck Split** — The division of Event cards into Early and Late sub-decks to ensure all cards drawn in the opening rounds are actionable.

**Late Deck** — The sub-deck of Event cards with preconditions not met at game start, merged into the Early deck at the start of Round 3.

**Nuclear Combo** — An unusually strong pairing of a Kingdom Advantage card and a Legacy card. The seeded Legacy card deal ensures that a counter-combination is always available to another player.

**Rivalry Pair** — Two Kingdom Advantage cards that naturally counter each other. The draft pool construction rules ensure that rivalry pairs are never separated.
